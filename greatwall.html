üìñ History of the Great Wall

The Great Wall of China is a symbol of China's historical strength and architectural prowess. Originally built to defend against northern invasions, construction began as early as the 7th century BC and continued for centuries. The most famous sections were built by the Ming dynasty between the 14th and 17th centuries. The wall stretches across deserts, mountains, and plains, with watchtowers, barracks, and fortresses along the route. The wall's purpose evolved over time, including border control, signal communication, and trade regulation. Despite being partially in ruins, many restored sections showcase the grandeur of this Wonder of the World. Today, it stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most visited landmarks globally.
